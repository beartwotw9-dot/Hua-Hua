App Design

This section presents key app flows and interaction decisions through selected mobile screens.

Discovery Map

The home view uses a map to surface real-time nearby resources, so users immediately see which stores, surplus types, and reservation slots are open — lowering search cost and embedding the platform into everyday routines.

Store List

List mode adds the comparison layer the map lacks, letting users quickly weigh distance, discount, item count, and rating, so the decision moves from a values-only impulse to a practical choice.

Item Details

The item page focuses on contents, pickup window, quantity limits, and a clear surplus note, addressing common uncertainty up front. The aim is not to pile on info but to remove last-mile hesitation.

Reserve & Pay

Reservation, payment, and pickup are integrated into one path to avoid drop-off when switching pages, and to give the merchant a cleaner reservation count. This step also continues the trust thread from earlier screens.

Personal Saved

The personal page tracks carbon savings, food-rescue counts, and participation history into a reviewable record — turning sustainability from a one-off purchase into something that accumulates and motivates return visits.

Merchant Console

The merchant side is more than item listings — it integrates inventory, exposure scheduling, and AI prep forecasting into one back-office workflow, making ResQfood not only a marketplace but an operational tool.
